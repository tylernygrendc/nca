Our Services

Chiropractic

Chiropractic is a licensed health care profession that addresses disorders of the musculoskeletal system. Most people see chiropractors for the treatment of back pain, neck pain, joint pain, and headaches. There is good evidence supporting its use for the treatment of acute low back pain.

Chiropractic treatments primarily involve controlled adjustments to spinal joints. These adjustments change the way the brain interprets incoming signals, which decreases pain and improves overall function. Chiropractors combine clinical knowledge with a physical examination of the joints of the body to determine if an adjustment is necessary. They will also screen for certain conditions that may make chiropractic adjustments unsafe, such as osteoporosis, bone cancers, and certain vascular diseases.

Local discomfort in the area of treatment is the most common side effect of chiropractic adjustments. Less common side effects include fatigue, headache, dizziness, and nausea.

Acupuncture

Acupuncture is a Traditional Chinese Medicine that involves the placement of thin needles at specific points on the body. Most people use acupuncture to treat pain conditions, headaches, and nausea. The American College of Physicians recommends acupuncture as a primary treatment for chronic low back pain.

Acupuncture is primarily concerned with the regulation of Qi, a metaphysical energy believed to flow in specific paths along the body. Although there is no physiologic basis for Qi, traditional acupuncture has been found to be more effective than placebo in the treatment of post-operative nausea and chronic low back pain.

Acupuncturists combine clinical knowledge with a physical examination of the body to determine an appropriate placement of needles. Needles are inserted a few millimeters into the skin, and are removed after a few minutes. The procedure is generally painless, although pinching, tingling, or itching sensations may occur. Common side effects of acupuncture include soreness, as well as minor bleeding or bruising at the side of insertion.

Dry Needling

Dry Needling is a modern therapy that reduces pain and improves range of motion in the short-term. It involves the insertion of needles into bands of tight muscle fibers called trigger points. Much like acupuncture, common side effects include soreness, as well as minor bleeding or bruising at the side of insertion.

Gua-Sha

Gua Sha is a Traditional Chinese Medicine that uses flat stones to apply gentle stroking pressure along muscles. It is primarily concerned with the regulation of Qi, a metaphysical energy believed to flow in specific paths along the body. Although there is no physiologic basis for Qi, modern health care providers have adopted the practice of Gua Sha to improve range of motion and to enhance healing after injury.

Bruising and tenderness in the area of treatment are common side effects of Gha Sha.

Cupping

Cupping is a Traditional Chinese Medicine that involves the gentle application of suction to the skin. It is primarily concerned with the regulation of Qi, a metaphysical energy believed to flow in specific paths along the body. Although there is no physiologic basis for Qi, cupping has been found to be beneficial in the treatment of chronic musculoskeletal conditions.

Bruising and tenderness in the area of treatment are common side effects of cupping.

Low-Level Laser

Cold laser accelerates the body’s rate of healing. It is applied directly to the problem area over a period of several minutes. Unlike comparable therapies, it doesn’t heat tissue in the treatment area. In the days following treatment, patients can expect reduced inflammation and pain.
